Muhammad Ali

The Greatest Heavyweight Boxer in the World

Here's a time line of Muhammad Ali's life:

  • 1942 - Born in Louisville, Kentucky. He was named Cassius Clay.
  • 1960 Ali wins the Light Heavyweight Gold medal at the Summer Olympics at Rome
  • 1963 - Ali wins the Heavyweight championship against Sonny Liston. After the fight he converts to the Nation Of Islam and changes his name to Muhammad Ali.
  • 1966 - Ali refuses the Mandatory draft for the Vietnam War citing his religious beliefs. His boxing license is revoked across all states.
  • 1970 - Ali was granted a license to box by the City of Atlanta Athletic Commission.
  • 1971 - Ali fights Joe Frazier for the heavyweight championship. A close fight but ultimately Ali suffers his first loss.
  • 1974 - Ali rematches with Joe Frazier and defeats him via unanimous decision.
  • 1974 - Ali fights against Undefeated Heavyweight George Foreman who previously defeated both Ken Norton and Joe Frazier. The bout is named "Rumble in the Jungle". Ali deploys his infamous "Rope-a-dope" tactic against Foreman and scores an 8th round KO against him to win the Heavyweight Championship again.
  • 1975 - Ali fights Joe Frazier again for the third time. Nicknamed "Thrilla in Manilla" Ali wins by TKO after Joe Frazier's trainer stops the fight after the 14th round.
  • 1976 - Ali wins a heavily contested decision against Ken Norton. Afterwards, he announces his retirement from boxing after converting to Sunni Islam from the Nation of Islam.
  • 1978 Ali fights Leon Spinks and wins his third heavyweight championship becoming first Heavyweight to win the belt three times.
  • 2016 - dies at the age of 74.
